What VERNEN™ does

S.o.C. Identification

Every legal document is governed by a Legal Standard of Creation — the statutes, rules of court, and professional codes that dictate what must appear, how it must be formatted, and what procedural requirements apply. VERNEN™ identifies the applicable S.o.C. before any analysis begins.

Protocol

The VERNEN S.o.C. Audit Protocol

A six-pass analytical framework that identifies the Legal Standard of Creation governing any document, then audits compliance against that standard with traceable findings.

01

S.o.C. Identification

Determine the governing statutes, rules of court, regulations, and professional codes that establish what the document must contain and how it must be structured.

02

Structural Integrity

Verify required sections, headings, formatting, signatures, verification clauses, and procedural prerequisites are present and correctly positioned.

03

Substantive Completeness

Confirm all mandatory factual allegations, legal elements, and supporting details required by the S.o.C. are addressed — not merely present but adequate.

04

Procedural Compliance

Check filing deadlines, service requirements, proof of service, fee waiver attachments, mandatory disclosures, and jurisdictional prerequisites.

05

Bias & Consistency

Detect one-sided framing, internal contradictions between document sections, unsupported conclusions, and deviations from the neutral standard the document should meet.

06

Evidentiary Sufficiency

Assess whether cited evidence supports stated conclusions, identify gaps between claims and proof, and flag unsubstantiated findings of fact.

Scope

Document coverage

Family Law

Dissolution petitions (FL-100), responses (FL-120), RFOs (FL-300/305/311), custody attachments, DV restraining orders (DV-100/110), income declarations.

Civil Rights

§ 1983 complaints, Monell claims, qualified immunity analysis, 42 U.S.C. § 1985 conspiracy, Bivens actions, state tort claims.

Court Orders

Custody orders, DV protective orders, contempt findings, judgment enforcement orders. Audited against CRC and statutory requirements.

Professional Conduct

Police reports (POST standards), custody evaluations (CRC 5.220), attorney conduct (CRPC), psychiatry evaluations (ABPN/APA), CPS determinations.

Administrative

State agency correspondence, SSA/DDS disability determinations, FCRA consumer reports, insurance claims handling, real estate transaction documents.

General Civil

Small claims (SC-100), appeals (APP-002), declarations (MC-031), fee waivers (FW-001/003), complaints, motions, and supporting memoranda.
